Did you know that many peoples' first exposure to programming is through Minecraft?

Hi, I'm Claire Thananopavarn, and I believe in the power of video games to teach. My first video game, Froggy Multiplication, was created to address pandemic learning inequalities and it won runner-up in the Congressional App Challenge. But even non-educational games teach too! On Steam alone, players logged 38 billion hours in a year. Hours of life in a virtual world informs our view of the real one, with the choices we make and the people we talk to. That's why I joined Girls Make Games to encourage underrepresented voices to join the industry.

I've learned so much at USC, and I try to pass on my knowledge. I've taught at Girls Make Games, made two educational video browser games, worked on computational science tools, and even coached gymnastics! (I have a secret double life as a stunt performer).

Minecraft first taught me to program, and now it's time for me to pay it forward. As a gameplay programmer, I want to use my talents for good, creating games for people to enjoy and learn from. Check my contact page, and let's team up!
